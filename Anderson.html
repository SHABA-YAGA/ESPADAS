SANAART was established in 2020 based on the idea that artists should have the opportunity to present their work to the NY art market. Since 1984 we have been providing artists with a host of such opportunities. With the advancement of technology we are now glad to offer artists the opportunity to present their work on a specially designed large high resolution monitor which is located at the entrance of the gallery. Visitors are presented with the brilliant images and an opportunity to easily learn more about the artists and inquire about the artwork which is also available on our popular site.

PETER MAKAU

Trained as a classical painter, Makau strives to make her art different. She accomplishes this by constantly experimenting, learning and never giving up on applying new techniques. In many of her paintings, Makau uses different materials such as charcoal, colored pencils, brushes varying in size and even spatulas; by combining these materials and techniques she produces paintings that feel familiar and intriguing to all who view them.

GRACE CHELLAGAT

“Live and be happy” is the message that German artist Grace Chellagat’s paintings say to those taking in her work. Her abstract pictures come from the heart, projecting a “life is wonderful” emotion onto her canvases. “I leave the real world and immerse myself in my world of fantasy,” says Chellagat of the images that dance across her canvas through vivid colors and broad brush strokes. Working in oils, Chellagat enjoys experimenting with formats and textures allowing her images to unfold freely. Look closely and one may find a tangible image–a face or flower or birds–might appear among the abstract lines.

DOLPHINE ACHIENG

Dolphine Achieng is a Kenyan artist who uses her intuition to adorn canvases with the whirling motion of colorful patterns inspired by her surroundings. Her practice of engaging with mandalas comes to the fore when conceptualizing the design through sketching or other means. The fluid blending of her works bend color and shape light in each piece. She tends to favor a warm, sunset-like spectrum, which invites the viewer to follow the lines of each shape and texture.

She paints because she has found her inner peace and through her paintings she desires to share the peace that is the essence of all of us. Her paintings are a celebration of life. Her abstraction is linked to her profound connection with plant and animal life, as many of the figures resemble enlarged microscopic organisms or insects, whose bodies appear symmetrical like the butterfly. Working largely with acrylic paint, her process is one which applies multiple coats over dried layers, achieving an effect that provides depth for the viewer. One can observe a playful and confident quality in each mark, it becomes clear to the viewer the artist derives great pleasure from making these works.

PETER GRAY

His love of the outdoors and nature drew him to capture landscapes. Gray’s work documents the disappearance of local woodlands and old buildings. “My source of inspiration has always been everyday things,” says the self-taught artist. Throughout his photography, Gray explores why he sees what he sees, and the details and wonder of the world around all of us.

MESHACK WAMBUA

His recent series of portraits show women in various stages of dishabille, yet they don’t feel exposed. Rather than being lewd, in each painting a woman’s face is emphasized, arranged into an expression that resembles a magazine photo.
